GOSH Meta-Regression

For every non-trivial subset of studies, re-fit a univariable meta-regression on a single continuous moderator and scatter the moderator coefficient against the within-subset I². Reveals how robust the moderator effect is to subset choice.

β × I² scatter

Reading the plot: a horizontal cloud of β values near zero means the moderator does not robustly predict effect size. A diagonal gradient from high-β / low-I² to low-β / high-I² indicates the moderator captures most of the heterogeneity. Vertical streaks suggest one or two outlier studies drive the moderator coefficient.
Adapted from Olkin-Dahabreh-Trikalinos GOSH (RSM 2012) with a meta-regression layer. Random-effects DL model; moderator coefficient β from weighted least squares with τ²-adjusted weights.
