PET-PEESE + Trim-and-Fill

Publication-bias adjustments. PET regresses the effect on SE; PEESE regresses on SE². The Stanley–Doucouliagos conditional procedure uses PET first and switches to PEESE if PET rejects H₀. Duval–Tweedie trim-and-fill imputes missing studies symmetric around the median.

Funnel plot with adjustments

PET: Stanley & Doucouliagos (2014) — TE = β₀ + β₁·SE + ε, weighted by 1/SE². PEESE: same model with SE² instead of SE. Trim-and-fill: Duval & Tweedie (2000), iterative L₀ / R₀ estimator, imputes k₀ studies reflected about the adjusted mean. Conditional PET-PEESE procedure follows Cochrane Handbook v6.5 §13.3.5.4 (small-study effects); trim-and-fill is positioned as a sensitivity analysis only, never the primary result, per Cochrane Handbook §13.3.5.3. Power is poor for k<10 — interpret with caution.
