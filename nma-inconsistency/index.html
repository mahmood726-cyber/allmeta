NMA Inconsistency

Detect inconsistency between direct and indirect evidence in network meta-analysis. Performs node-splitting (Dias et al. 2010) on every comparison with a closed loop, and a global design-by-treatment test (Higgins et al. 2012).

Net-split forest

Reading the plot: direct and indirect whiskers should overlap for a consistent network. Non-overlapping whiskers with |z| > 1.96 indicate a specific loop with inconsistency. The global design-by-treatment test is the omnibus check.
Node-splitting: Dias S, Welton NJ, Caldwell DM, Ades AE (Stat Med 2010). Design-by-treatment inconsistency model: Higgins JPT, Jackson D, Barrett JK, et al. (Res Synth Methods 2012). This tool performs the simplified IV-weighted version of both; dedicated NMA software gives fuller output.
