GOSH plot — all-subset meta-analysis

Graphical display of study heterogeneity (Olkin, Dahabreh, Trikalinos 2012). For every non-empty subset of studies, compute the pooled estimate and I² under a fixed-effect (or DL random-effects) model. Each subset becomes one dot; clusters suggest influential studies or subgroup structure.

Interpretation: multimodal clustering indicates subgroup heterogeneity (the meta-analysis averages two or more distinct populations). A thick "horizontal band" at one estimate means most subsets converge on the same answer — homogeneous evidence. Streaks along the I² axis often indicate one or two highly influential outliers.
Olkin I, Dahabreh IJ, Trikalinos TA. GOSH - a graphical display of study heterogeneity. Res Synth Methods 2012;3(3):214-23.
